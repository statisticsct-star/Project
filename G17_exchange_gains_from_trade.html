What the welfare theorems mean here
  • Gains from trade: if both utility indices are above 100, the current allocation makes both traders better off than the initial endowment.
  • Contract curve: points on the green curve satisfy equal marginal rates of substitution, so there is no further mutually beneficial trade left.
  • First welfare theorem: in this competitive-exchange setup, the market-clearing allocation implied by the equilibrium price ratio lies on the contract curve.
  • Second welfare theorem: changing the initial endowment changes the competitive equilibrium point, showing how redistribution can alter which efficient allocation the market supports.

Interpretation note
This case is best read as a stylized exchange-theory translation. The Austrian data tell us that trade integration deepened substantially after EU accession and eastern enlargement. The Edgeworth box then turns that evidence into a clean teaching sequence about endowments, gains from trade, competitive equilibrium, and Pareto efficiency.
