Expected-utility logic in this graph
  • Expected wealth: the probability-weighted average of the two wealth states, shown by the gray guide.
  • Certainty equivalent: the certain wealth level that gives the same utility as the risky lottery.
  • Risk premium: expected wealth − certainty equivalent. It is positive for a concave utility curve.
  • Insurance choice: buy the contract when its premium is below the maximum willingness to pay implied by expected utility.

Interpretation note
This case should be read as a stylized expected-utility calibration, not as a literal underwriting model for a named household. The official Australian data anchor the premium levels, flood-risk context, and coverage gap. The utility curve then translates those facts into a clean teaching sequence about certainty equivalents, risk premia, and insurance demand.
