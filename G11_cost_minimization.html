Production versus domestic sales
Production fell sharply while Australian vehicle sales stayed high, so domestic demand was increasingly met by imports.
2012 output per major producer versus efficient scale
The Productivity Commission reported that cost-competitive scale for most assembly plants was about 200,000–300,000 vehicles a year, but individual Australian plants were well below that range.
Holden’s reported cost gap versus Asian plants
Holden reported an average cost gap of $3,750 per car relative to some GM plants in Asia: $2,000 from Australian input costs, $1,500 from local-supplier components, and $250 from imported-component logistics.
