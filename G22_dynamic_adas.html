How to read the graph: in the dynamic AD–AS model, inflation stays near target when nominal demand grows at roughly the same pace as potential output plus the inflation target. If demand runs ahead of that pace, DAD shifts out faster than LRAS and the economy overheats. If demand runs behind it, or policy tightens strongly, inflation falls and output can slip below potential.

How to interpret dynamic AD–AS
  • Balanced growth: if nominal demand growth roughly equals capacity growth plus the inflation target, DAD and DSRAS keep intersecting LRAS close to the target band.
  • Overheating: if demand growth runs faster than that benchmark, the output gap turns positive and inflation rises above target.
  • Disinflation: if demand growth slows relative to capacity, or policy deliberately restrains DAD, inflation falls; output may move below potential during the cooling phase.
  • Supply shocks: adverse supply shifts lift inflation and reduce output at the same time, making the inflation-output trade-off harder than in a pure demand cycle.

Pre-pandemic baseline Annual CPI was 1.8% in Dec 2019, GDP was up 2.2% through the year, unemployment was 5.1%, and the cash rate was 0.75% Used as the starting benchmark: modest growth, inflation below the midpoint of target, and no large positive output gap.
Reopening rebound By Dec 2021 GDP was 3.4% above Dec 2019, annual CPI was 3.5%, unemployment was 4.2%, and the cash rate was 0.10% Anchors the step where DAD rebounds strongly and inflation moves above target while capacity is still catching up.
Overheating / peak inflation In Dec 2022 GDP was up 2.7% through the year, annual CPI was 7.8%, unemployment was 3.5%, and the cash rate rose to 3.10% Mapped into a hot economy with inflation far above target and output around or above potential.
Disinflation begins In Dec 2023 GDP grew 1.5% through the year, annual CPI fell to 4.1%, and the cash rate had reached 4.35% Shows the turn from overheating toward disinflation as tighter policy restrains demand and inflation starts to come down.
Return to target range In Dec 2024 GDP grew 1.3% through the year, annual CPI fell to 2.4%, unemployment was 4.0%, and the cash rate remained 4.35% Anchors the final step where inflation moves back inside target and output growth is much slower than in the reopening surge.
Interpretation note
This is best read as a stylized dynamic-macro teaching sequence. The official Australian data show below-target inflation before the pandemic, above-target inflation during the reopening and 2022 price surge, and substantial disinflation by late 2024. The dynamic AD–AS diagram turns that evidence into a compact classroom story about trend growth, overheating, and the cooling phase required to return inflation to target.

Source anchors used: ABS Consumer Price Index releases for December 2019, 2021, 2022, 2023, and 2024; ABS National Accounts releases for December 2019 to 2024; ABS Labour Force releases for December 2019, 2021, 2022, and 2024; and RBA cash-rate decisions and cash-rate history.
