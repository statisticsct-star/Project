Model logic
Inverse demand: P = a - bQ
Marginal revenue: MR = a - 2bQ
Marginal cost: MC = c + dQ
Average total cost: ATC = F / Q + c + 0.5dQ
Competition and MC regulation choose the efficient quantity where P = MC.
Monopoly chooses the output where MR = MC. AC regulation sets price high enough for the firm to break even, so P = ATC at the regulated quantity.

Why this is a monopoly-and-regulation case
Australia Post’s reserved ordinary letter services are a statutory monopoly. The ACCC and the Minister oversee price changes because competitive pressure is not strong enough to discipline price on its own. The case therefore gives students a real-world example of a monopolist that is also regulated rather than left free to choose its static profit-maximising price.
How to read the graph and charts together
The main graph is stylised: it shows the intuition that repeated leftward demand shifts and rising average cost make a fixed-price network harder to sustain. The two charts next to it use official price and quantity data, so students can connect the stylised monopoly-regulation picture to observed movements in stamp prices and letter volumes.
