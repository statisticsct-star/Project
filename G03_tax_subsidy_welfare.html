Key teaching points
Taxes A per-unit tax creates a wedge between the buyer price and the seller price. Government revenue is the wedge times the traded quantity.
Subsidies A subsidy increases the traded quantity but costs the government money. The subsidy rectangle is a budget cost, not a welfare gain by itself.
Deadweight loss The gray triangle marks the loss in potential total surplus caused by the wedge driving quantity away from the no-policy equilibrium for the same underlying demand and supply curves.

What the checker looks for
Curve logic Taxes on buyers shift demand down; taxes on sellers shift supply up; subsidies to buyers shift demand up; subsidies to sellers shift supply down.
Welfare logic Taxes raise government revenue but reduce total surplus and create deadweight loss. Subsidies lower government balance and also create deadweight loss if they push quantity above the no-policy equilibrium.
